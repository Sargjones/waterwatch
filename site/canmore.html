Canmore water supply
Surface water (~50%) Rundle Forebay — fed by Spray Lakes reservoir via TransAlta canal system from Kananaskis Country
Groundwater (~50%) Shallow aquifer beneath downtown Canmore, hydrologically connected to the Bow River
Operator EPCOR Water Services under utility management agreement with the Town of Canmore (expires 2030)
Treatment Pumphouse 1 (groundwater, chlorination) · Pumphouse 2 / Rundle Forebay (coagulation, gravity filtration, UV)
Rundle Forebay levels are not publicly available — EPCOR operates on a SCADA system with no public data feed. The Spray River at Banff is the best available proxy for forebay supply health.

Bow Basin snowpack
The Bow River Basin snowpack at spring peak was 68% of the historical normal — below average but not at crisis level. The Bow River at Banff discharge of ~145 cms reflects active snowmelt in the upper Rockies. This will taper significantly by late July as snowpack is exhausted and summer drought sets in.

Year-round conservation — Canmore

High impact
Know your water source by street
North of the Trans-Canada Highway — groundwater from downtown wells. South of the Bow River and central Canmore — Rundle Forebay surface water. Each has different drought vulnerability.
High impact
Watch the Bow River level in July–August
Once snowmelt ends (typically late July), the Bow River drops significantly. Low Bow River flow at Banff is an early signal of potential restrictions. This dashboard will show it.
High impact
Don't swim in the Rundle Forebay
The Rundle Forebay supplies roughly half of Canmore's drinking water. Swimming, dogs, and recreational use increase treatment costs and risk. Signage exists but is poorly enforced.
Medium impact
Water gardens before 7am
Canmore's dry mountain air means evaporation rates are higher than coastal cities. Early morning watering loses 30% less water to evaporation than midday watering.
Medium impact
Report leaks to EPCOR immediately
Canmore had 23% municipal water loss at its best in recent years — leaks in the aging distribution system are a persistent issue. Report at 403.609.6400.
Medium impact
Sign up for Voyent Alert
The Town of Canmore uses Voyent Alert for emergency notifications including water restrictions. Free to sign up at canmore.ca — you'll know about restrictions before your neighbour does.
