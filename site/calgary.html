Infrastructure Context — Bearspaw South Feeder Main

⚠ Two catastrophic failures in 18 months — and a $342M reckoning

The Bearspaw South Feeder Main is the most critical single pipe in Calgary's water network, carrying approximately 60% of the city's treated drinking water from the Bearspaw Water Treatment Plant into the distribution system. Installed in the mid-1970s, the prestressed concrete cylinder pipe (PCCP) was first flagged as a high-consequence risk in 2004 — but repeated recommendations to inspect, monitor, and rehabilitate it were deferred across multiple councils and management cycles over 20 years.

2004
Risk first formally identified after a feeder main rupture in northeast Calgary
2017 · 2020 · 2022
Three separate recommendations to inspect the Bearspaw feeder main deferred or redirected
June 2024
First catastrophic rupture floods Trans-Canada Highway; city begins concrete encasement repairs and installs acoustic fibre-optic monitoring (1 million sensing wires)
December 30, 2025
Second catastrophic failure — same main, ~1 km from June 2024 break. Pipe splits in a "zipper" failure with no warning wire snaps in preceding two months. 13 people rescued from icy floodwater. City-wide conservation order issued.
January 2026
Independent panel report released: city ignored 20+ years of warning signs; problems span multiple councils and management teams. City council votes unanimously to implement all recommendations.
April 2026
Emergency reinforcement completed. Parallel replacement main under active construction, expected complete by end of 2026.
2027–2030
$342M remediation program proposed — nearly double the $168M spent 2023–2026. City requesting provincial cost-sharing.

The dual-source design of Calgary's water system — Bearspaw WTP drawing from the Bow, Glenmore WTP drawing from the Elbow — is both a strength and a vulnerability. When Bearspaw is compromised, the Elbow/Glenmore system cannot fully compensate for the whole city. This is why the flow data at 05BH004 and 05BJ001 is not just environmental monitoring: it is directly linked to Calgary's most pressing infrastructure story.
