Infrastructure Context — Bearspaw South Feeder Main

Two catastrophic failures in 18 months — and a $342M reckoning

The Bearspaw South Feeder Main is the most critical single pipe in Calgary's water network, carrying approximately 60% of the city's treated drinking water from the Bearspaw Water Treatment Plant into the distribution system. Installed in the mid-1970s as prestressed concrete cylinder pipe (PCCP), it was first flagged as a high-consequence risk in 2004 — but recommendations to inspect, monitor, and rehabilitate it were deferred across multiple councils and management cycles for 20 years.

2004
Risk formally identified after a feeder main rupture in northeast Calgary
2017 · 2020 · 2022
Three separate recommendations to inspect the Bearspaw feeder main deferred or redirected
June 2024
First catastrophic rupture floods Trans-Canada Highway. City begins concrete encasement repairs and installs acoustic fibre-optic monitoring (1 million sensing wires).
December 30, 2025
Second catastrophic failure ~1 km from the June 2024 break. Pipe splits in a "zipper" failure with no warning. 13 people rescued from icy floodwater. City-wide conservation order issued.
January 2026
Independent panel report: city ignored 20+ years of warning signs. Council votes unanimously to implement all recommendations.
April 2026
Emergency reinforcement completed. Parallel replacement main under active construction.
2027–2030
$342M remediation program proposed — nearly double the $168M spent 2023–2026. Provincial cost-sharing requested.

Calgary's dual-source design — Bearspaw WTP drawing from the Bow, Glenmore WTP from the Elbow — is both a strength and a vulnerability. When Bearspaw is compromised, the Elbow/Glenmore system cannot fully compensate. This is why the flow readings at 05BH004 and 05BJ001 are not just environmental monitoring: they are directly linked to Calgary's most pressing infrastructure story.
