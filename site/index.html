How WaterWatch works

Live hydrometric data River flow and water level from Environment Canada's Water Survey (WSC) network — updated every 6 hours via GitHub Actions. The same data federal and provincial agencies use for flood forecasting and drought monitoring.
Data gaps are shown, not hidden Reservoir levels, industrial water use, and groundwater data are rarely published publicly. Every section exists on every city page — unknown data is labelled "not publicly available," not omitted. This is intentional accountability pressure.
Infrastructure stories, not just numbers Calgary's feeder main failures, Edmonton's no-reservoir design, Victoria's 110-year-old Sooke Flowline, Charlottetown's groundwater dependency — the risk stories that don't appear in municipal annual reports.
